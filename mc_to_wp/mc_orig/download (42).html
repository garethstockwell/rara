Record of recent open meeting of residents
Dear Resident
 
We wanted to update you on the open meeting held a month ago.  If you were at the meeting or sent apologies you should have received the full meeting record.  If not, and you would like the full record then please let us know.  We will also send a second mailing with the Riverside Vision draft document.
 
We discussed:
 
Greater Cambridge Local Plan
 
This is in preparation by officers and the wording of the policies will determine the planning and other policy decisions that affect us for the next 5 - 15 years.  There will be further consultations later in the year, probably in the Autumn.  There are specific policies for the Eastern Gate (Newmarket Road) and River Cam corridor that directly affect us.  We feel it is important that we have input to these policies as experience has shown that, if they are not precisely worded, developers can drive a coach and horses through good intentions.
 
·         Volunteers are invited to join Al Hanagan, Lynette Gilbert and other interested members to develop and work up Greater Cambridge Local Plan policy contributions/inputs on behalf of RARA: these can be shared and specified as requested with the City Council.
 
 
A Vision for Cambridge Riverside
 
In 2010 the City Council endorsed an outline vision to “create a pedestrian and cycle priority route linking the green spaces of Stourbridge Common and Midsummer Common”, a “pleasant promenade” which would “accommodate walkers and cyclists in safety’.  But this did not make it into the current Local Plan.  We are trying to obtain funding to extend this idea and incorporate it into the thinking of officers at County and City.  The attached document indicates the concept.  One way forward might be to include this in a "Neighbourhood Plan" that would have legal status on a par with the Greater Cambridge Local Plan.  It's something many communities are working on.  However, this is resource intensive.  There is some financial support from government to help preparation.  There are also some alternative funding possibilities such as using developer contributions and public art funds.
 
Actions on A Vision for Cambridge Riverside
 
·         As above – volunteers are invited to work further on the Riverside Vision with David Speigalhalter and Al Hanagan and other interested members; and ideas and suggestions from all much welcomed.
·         Al and colleagues will arrange a meeting with John Dixon, Planning Policy Manager at City Council, to progress input to the Greater Cambridge Local Plan, the Riverside Vision, and to find out more about the pros and cons of neighbourhood planning for some point in the future.
·         Further follow up with Councillor Bennett on Art opportunities.
 
Parking and EV Charging
Lynette Gilbert updated on the position regarding the earlier consultation on EV charging points.  RARA has been active in feeding back to the County Council, including attending site visits and submissions.  Subsequently, a delegated decision-making meeting was held on 4th April.  Highways Officers were persuaded to make a few of the improvements we suggested where we were represented by County Councillor Alex Bulat. 
 
She wrote "I managed to get one of the many concessions, which is restricting the residents spaces to resident EV only. I hope this will minimise the traffic impact at least on Beche Road.  This was really challenging as Councillors were informed at the very start of the meeting that the decision only relates to changes in parking restrictions not the detail of the scheme that was approved before either Cllr Bird or I were County Councillors. So although there was discussion on Riverside, the decision itself was not on locations. At least it is mentioned in writing. I think a lot of the issues with this scheme have been as it is and was treated as a pilot without an EV strategy for the country so I spoke already with colleagues on Highways asking them to work on an EV strategy so the same issues don't happen again as well as reviewing how consultation is conducted and the decision making process.  I am sorry we could not make the scheme much better in this final stage, but there are some very minor concessions, an acknowledgement that the points can be removed if funding for the Riverside vision is found, and we have a dialogue open on the strategy points at least."
 
If anyone is prepared to co-ordinate a traffic and parking interest group under the RARA umbrella please contact us as these issues are a continual problem.
 
easyHotel
 
As residents are aware work on the EH site at the top of Godesdone Road was suspended in September 2021.  This followed the demolition of No. 2 Godesdone Road which was contrary to planning consent.  Al Hanagan has been in constant liaison with John Shuttleworth at the City Council (Planning Enforcement) over the last eight months to express residents’ deep concerns on how and why the demolition could have come about at all, the implications, and what will happen next.  He was advised that the council is investigating the matter, and John Shuttlesworth’s report will go to the City Council Legal team for opinion.  Despite the rumour that the new owner of the site will have to re-apply for full planning permission, Al has not received any response to his requests for information so we don't know for sure what will happen.
 
Call for Volunteers
 
Caroline Neville and the team urged those neighbours who may wish to offer assistance with the work of the association, and their wider RA community colleagues, to come forward – a cordial invitation is extended to all those potential and budding volunteers who would like to get involved.
 
There are opportunities of all shapes and sizes, from RARA secretary (where there is a vacancy) to joining the Steering Group, or leading on a specific theme, issue or topic for a time limited period. If it is not possible to join a group but you would like to support or advise, or offer expertise at the end of the phone this is always helpful.  RARA’s current priority, for example, is around developing and maintaining a new web site.  If you would like to contribute in any way, please do get in touch with any member of the Steering Group.  It is also suggested that a social gathering/event will help further connections and  networking between everyone.
 
Kind Regards
 
RARA Steering Group
