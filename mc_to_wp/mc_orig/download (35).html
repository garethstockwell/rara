Introduction of controlled crossing as part of wider Newmarket Road improvements
View this email in your browser
The package of work to improve Newmarket Road from the Airport to the Elizabeth Way roundabout has just been published for consultation. The consultation deadline is 20th March 2023.  Would you please have a look at the proposals and tell County Highways what you think.

 

As a representative of RARA (Mike Evans) attended a Teams meeting on 20th January with the County Highways team facilitated by our County Councillor Alex Bulat.  It is understood that work is expected to start in a year’s time.
A summary of the proposals out for consultation, together with some initial observations, follows.

  • From the Riverside residents’ point of view, a most significant proposed change is the remodelling of the River Lane/ Newmarket Road / Coldhams Lane junction to include controlled pedestrian crossings at all four entry points.  This is a big win, RARA have complained about the danger of crossing Newmarket Road here for ages.
  • The plan includes removing the central barrier, apart from a few refuges, and have a single lane each way for cars from the Elizabeth Way roundabout to the Coldhams Lane junction.   There will be a dedicated separated cycle lane, in the same direction as car travel in each carriageway.  This means the footpath on the South of Newmarket Road will no longer be shared between cyclists and pedestrians, another win.
  • At the Elizabeth Way junction,  a rather complex “Cyclops” roundabout is proposed. The pedestrian under passes will be filled in.  There will be controlled pedestrian and cycle crossings at each entrance to the roundabout.  Bikes will be able to go both ways round the roundabout.  The current bus stop going east will be moved nearer to the roundabout as a “floating” bus stop like those in Hills Road.  The current controlled crossing at Abbey Street will be removed.  Bikes wanting to turn right will have to go down the roundabout and then cross and return on the other carriageway.  The current citybound bus stop near the roundabout will be removed and a “floating” bus stop put near the Travelodge.
  • Near the entrance to Godesdone Road, the eastbound carriageway will split into two lanes, one straight ahead and one to turn right to Coldhams Lane.  The entrance to Godesdone Road will have a continuous paved and raised path for cyclists and pedestrians.  The idea is to slow down cars turning right to enter Godesdone Road.
  • There will be controlled pedestrian crossings at the traffic lights controlling each of the four entrances to the junction.  However, there is no plan to yellow box the junction, which would, if enforced, stop the gridlock problem that is a significant cause of people jumping the lights.  Similarly, there are no plans to stop taxis dropping off and picking up either in front of the Travelodge, or on the north side between Godesdone Road and River Lane.  The issue that this staggered junction confuses motorists does not seem to have been given much thought.
  • Westbound traffic at the River Lane/ Newmarket Road part of the junction wanting to turn right into River Lane will have an area past the lights where a few vehicles could wait until the fast traffic coming the other way ceases and then they can turn right. 
  • The effect of this and other changes, particularly to the Elizabeth Way roundabout, will be to reduce the road capacity. This leads to the expectation that motorists living to the East of Cambridge are likely to oppose these changes. As ever, it is those who don’t like something proposed who are more likely to engage with the consultation than those who like the proposal.

Would you please have a look at the proposals and tell County Highways what you think?
 
The consultation can be found at GCP Newmarket Road improvements and Park & Ride relocation | Consult Cambridgeshire (engagementhq.com)

The consultation call says:
Phase 1: Newmarket Road Improvements and Park & Ride public engagement programme kicks off today (23 January 2023)
The Greater Cambridge Partnership is consulting on the proposed design for walking, cycling and public transport improvements on Newmarket Road and the selection of a preferred site for the relocated Park & Ride.
Newmarket Road provides access into Cambridge from the east, is a popular destination in its own right, accommodates the day-to-day journeys made by local residents and is a high frequency bus route. Newmarket Road suffers from substantial congestion particularly during peak travel times, provides a poor quality environment and unattractive infrastructure for people travelling on foot and by bicycle
The consultation includes proposals for transformational changes to Newmarket Road to provide high-quality footways, crossings, segregated cycle tracks, bus lanes and junction improvements. The proposals include relocating and expanding the Newmarket Road Park & Ride site.
There will be an online event on Wednesday 8 February and two public drop-in events on 15 February and 1 March. You can register for the virtual event here.
To find out more and share your views with us please visit the following website:
GCP Newmarket Road improvements and Park & Ride relocation | Consult Cambridgeshire (engagementhq.com)
Your feedback will be considered as part of further design and planning work and ongoing engagement with landowners along the route.
This will give communities an opportunity to shape how this will look, aiming to provide better walking and cycling connections.
