18 January, 2022

Dear Residents,
 
You may remember a communication in October about a joint City and County Council proposal to install Electric Vehicle (EV) Charging Points in the Riverside area.  County Highways has now issued a Traffic Regulation Order (TRO) which specifies the proposed locations. The TRO consultation closes on January 31st.

We are sure many residents support the need to create the infrastructure for renewable modes of transport. However, we have some concerns about the current proposals. These are summarised at the end of this note.

Where will EV charging points be located?
  • 15 ‘slow charge’ 7KW bays are proposed on Beche Road, Priory Road, River Lane and Riverside. These points will take 6-8 hours to deliver a full charge,
  • 2 rapid charge on-street 50KW bays are proposed on Beche Road. These points will take 1-2 hours to deliver a full charge.
3 further charging bays will be provided in Abbey Road car park (two 7KW and one 50KW). County Highways will publish these in a future TRO.
 How will the chargepoints work? What will they cost?
  • All points will be operated by BP Pulse (https://bpchargemaster.com/)
  • Any EV owner can use them, either on a subscription or pay-as-you go basis.
  • Rapid 50KW points will be charged at commercial rates. Penalty fees will automatically be charged if users stay on for longer than 90 minutes.
  • The 7KW points will have no time limits or penalties. County Highways believe the 6-8 hour charging time will make these points unattractive to non-resident EV owners. 7KW charging costs will be capped at the ‘low to medium’ end of commercial rates.
What physical infrastructure is involved?
  • A 2 vehicle charging bay is served by one charging pillar with 2 charge points. 3 and 4 vehicle bays will each have two charging pillars.
  • The 7KW charging pillars are just under 140cm high – see visuals below, including a County Council mock-up of how one 7KW point on Beche Road might look.
  • The 50KW rapid pillars are taller, around 200cm.
All pillars will be black, based on Conservation Officer advice, and will be in place for a minimum of 7 years, extendable to 10 years.
BP stock images; County Council photomontage

Our concerns:

  1. Six residents’ parking bays will be lost on Beche Road, despite resident spaces being very limited. County Highways view is that converting 11 pay & display spaces will create additional overnight parking for residents who own EVs.
     
  2. EV drivers from outside the Riverside area (e.g. hotel guests) could use the 7KW bays as free daytime parking in the CPZ, since there are no time limits or overstay penalties.
     
  3. Concentrating 7 bays around the Cellarer’s Chequer will potentially increase vehicle movements and congestion at that one junction.
     
  4. Converting 2 riverfront pay & display bays to charging points will increase vehicle activity on a busy cycle and pedestrian route. Installing new street furniture could prejudice the City’s vision of a cycle and pedestrian-friendly ‘boulevarde’ along all of Riverside.
     
  5. Pavements are narrow in most proposed locations, but the mock-up shows the pillar on the pavement. A minimum 1.5m pavement width is essential for disabled access.
     
  6. The long term County Highways strategy is unclear – will they continue to take away resident parking bays as demand for EV charging increases? Could residents be allowed to run cables across the footway from our houses to the road instead.

We believe a better solution might include:

  • Relocating some Beche Rd/Priory Rd bays and the riverfront bays to less contested areas, such as the Abbey Road car park or Walnut Tree Avenue.
     
  • Restricting 7KW points to resident parking permit holders and/or introducing penalties for remaining on the point long after a full charge is achieved.
     
  • Reducing the number of points installed in our residential streets, since there are already 4 free-to-use 7KW points in the Tesco car park; and campaigning for more points to be located in this and other retailer car parks locally.
We would welcome feedback from residents on the proposed locations and approach.

Also, if you are thinking of buying a vehicle needing a charging point in the next three years or so, could you please let us know?  Having a picture of the likely demand is the best way to use this opportunity to residents’ advantage.

Please submit your views before January 31st to County Highways on: https://www.cambridgeshire.gov.uk/residents/travel-roads-and-parking/roads-and-pathways/traffic-regulation-orders/permanent-traffic-regulation-orders#proposed-tro--reference-number-pr--------de-freville-avenue-and-riverside-areas--cambridge-6-0
 

Kind Regards,
