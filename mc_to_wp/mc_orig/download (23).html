The next Community Sunday meet-up will be at 4pm on Sunday 1st October 2023
in the Pye Building at the Cambridge Museum of Technology (Cheddar’s Lane entrance)

At 5pm, we will hold the Riverside Area Residents' Association (RARA)
Annual General Meeting at the same location.

All residents are warmly invited to attend both events!

Riverside Area Community Sunday – 4pm Sunday 1st October, Pye Building

Following three successful Community Sunday events this summer, we’re pleased to announce that we can continue to meet through Autumn and Winter (first Sunday of every month), thanks to Cambridge Museum of Technology.

So, our next community gathering of friends and neighbours is at 4pm on Sunday 1st October in the
Pye Building at the Cambridge Museum of Technology, which houses the Pye Exhibition.

Please bring yourselves, your children and neighbours for our informal get together.  Feel free to bring some snacks and something to drink - we will all share.

Note that you will need to enter the Museum at the Cheddars Lane entrance (just next to Tesco) as the Riverside gate is locked at 4pm.

We look forward to seeing you!

RARA Annual General Meeting – 5pm Sunday 1st October, Pye Building

Following the Community Sunday gathering we will hold our AGM, also at the Pye Building.  This will be brief (aiming for no more than 45 minutes).  We would like to:

  • Introduce the friends and neighbours who are working hard co-ordinating our various themes and sub-groups. The Museum will also update us on its activities.
  • Confirm governance arrangements (Steering Group, Treasurer and so on); and report on RARA’s financial position. 

We are not asking for volunteers (though we’re always ready to accept new help on the Steering Group so please don’t hold back!), or for money.   We would, however, really like to strengthen our network of street representatives who flag issues of local concern and help distribute information. And we are always on the look out for creative ideas for community activities and for fundraising.

RARA has several voluntary sub-groups (‘coalitions of the willing’) currently, including:
  • A Vision for Riverside – including improvements for pedestrians and cyclists from Priory Road to Stourbridge Common; parking; and GCP plans for three new Greenways to converge on Riverside at the Stourbridge Common entrance
  • Coopers Furniture site – liaising with the new owners of the site
  • The future of the Abbey Church and the adjacent Logic House site and garages
  • Cambridge Museum of Technology – collaborative working
  • Major consultations e.g., the Grafton Centre, the Beehive, Cambridge Eastern Access
    (GCP Newmarket Road proposals)
  • Communications and IT – including work on a new website

We hope to see you on 1st October!
