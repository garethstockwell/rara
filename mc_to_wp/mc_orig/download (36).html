Brief Summary of Information from Meeting
View this email in your browser

Record of Meeting to update residents on the status of the Abbey Church.

The Christchurch Parish hosted a meeting on the evening of 11th October to update residents on the status of the Abbey Church (also known as St Andrew-the-Less) on Newmarket Road.
This church is no longer used by the parish as it is small, lacks heating and other facilities and requires major repairs. Christchurch has therefore started the process of “closure” (historically called “deconsecration”) which means that the building can be used for other purposes besides worship.  Once closed, responsibility for the building will be transferred to the Diocese of Ely, who will look for potential buyers for the site (church building plus churchyard).
In the short term, this does not change a lot.  The parish has been informally seeking alternative good uses for the building for over 20 years.  Options looked at include a nursery; a community centre; a nursery; a counselling centre and combinations of all these. None of the options could generate sufficient funds to repair and maintain the building.
Looking ahead, the situation is complex. 
  1. The building is Grade 2 listed and rated “at risk” by English Heritage.  The gable walls are a particular concern.  While both the parish and diocese will make the minimum repairs necessary to keep the building safe, proper repairs are likely to cost £750k. To adapt the building to a new use would probably cost up to £1m. There is no sewerage or gas on the site, and no vehicle access.
  2. Only the building is the responsibility of the parish/diocese.  The churchyard – which is still consecrated ground – is the responsibility of the city council.  There is also a ‘panhandle’ of land behind Logic House which forms part of the site but is of uncertain status.
  3. It’s not clear how the diocese would seek an innovative new use, or what resources they can dedicate to the search.  The committee responsible is made up of volunteers and they are likely to appoint an Estate Agent to market the property.  Realistically the most financially attractive outcome will be to sell the site and building to a developer.  The vacant Logic House is likely to be a factor as this would increase the development opportunity.    Demolition is unlikely but could be a possibility.  However, the diocese say that their preference is to find a financially viable use which preserves the building, ideally retaining some community use.
Steve Midgley, the previous vicar of the parish, continues to be the person to contact if you have any great ideas (or know anyone with £1m+ to invest).  His email is steve@cccam.org.uk.
