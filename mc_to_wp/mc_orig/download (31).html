Newmarket Road Improvements Consultation

Update and Next Steps


Further to our bulletin dated 8th February this is a reminder to our neighbours that the final closing date for comments on the proposed changes to Newmarket Road is fast approaching.  The closing date for comments is 20th March 2023.

We can also advise that an additional ‘drop in’ consultation has been arranged by the Greater Cambridge Partnership for 7th March.  They say:
 
Additional Event

We would like to thank those who have already engaged with us during our current Cambridge Eastern Access consultation. If you haven't done so already, we encourage you to complete our survey. You can find it at https://consultcambs.uk.engagementhq.com/gcp-newmarket-road-23

Greater Cambridge Partnership will be holding an additional in-person drop-in event on Tuesday 7 March 2023 at Anglia Ruskin University, East Road, Cambridge, CB1 1PT within the Lord Ashcroft Building. Our team will be there between 5:30-7:30pm to answer any questions you may have. 

Our consultation will be closing on Monday 20 March at 12 noon.

A number of people have sent concerns or observations to us that you might like to consider when making your comments:
  • The current controlled crossing for bikes and pedestrians at Abbey Street will be removed.  Bikes going north wanting to turn right will have to go left to the roundabout and then cross and return back on the other carriageway.  The highways team say there are very few such cases and it will be safer for them to go down New Street and cross Newmarket Road at the new controlled crossing at Coldhams Lane. Will this work?
  • The current citybound bus stop near the roundabout will be removed and a “floating” bus stop put near the Travelodge.  Might this be better outside Beacon House?
  • Just before the entrance to Godesdone Road, the eastbound carriageway will split into two lanes, one straight ahead and one to turn right to Coldhams Lane.  The entrance to Godesdone Road will have a continuous paved and raised path for cyclists and pedestrians.  The idea is to slow down cars turning left to enter Godesdone Road.  While it is true that cars turn in too fast, the highway danger is caused by cars not turning tailgating and there are no plans to improve the parking controls at the entrance which, with the blind corner, create a surprise.  The consequent sudden movements are dangerous.  Does this junction need a rethink?
  • At the Elizabeth Way roundabout, they propose a rather complex “Cyclops” roundabout.  The pedestrian under passes will be filled in.  There will be controlled pedestrian and cycle crossings at each entrance to the roundabout.  Bikes will be able to go both ways round the roundabout.  This, together with a similar roundabout at the McDonalds near Wadloes Road, are the two factors which are likely to reduce the traffic capacity of Newmarket Road.  These designs reflect the new national policy to give priority on highways first to pedestrians, second to cyclists, third to public transport and lastly to cars.  Does this design adequately balance the interests of these conflicting groups?
  • At the River Lane / Newmarket Road / Coldhams Lane junction, there will be controlled pedestrian crossings at the traffic lights controlling each of the four entrances to the junction.  At present this is a junction with many cases of light jumping.  The causes for this are complex but one is traffic blocking entrances to the junction.  The issue that this staggered junction confuses motorists does not seem to have been given much thought.  Similarly, there are no plans to stop taxis dropping off and picking up either in front of the Travelodge, or on the north side between Godesdone Road and River Lane.  Should there be a plan to “yellow box” the junction with enforcement cameras?
  • Westbound traffic at the River Lane / Newmarket Road part of the junction wanting to turn right into River Lane will have an area past the lights where one or two vehicles could wait until the fast traffic coming the other way ceases and then they can turn right.  Will this be satisfactory?

Other thoughts and questions are continuing to flow through to us, so please do feel free in sending feedback on any issues and observations you would like to share to Riverside.Cambs@gmail.com

We intend to send out a further brief update and reminder to Riverside Area residents a few days before the final cut-off date.

We do urge however, if you can, to respond on a personal and direct basis to the Greater Cambridge Partnership itself by 20th March at noon.  The GCP link for easy reference and access is
https://consultcambs.uk.engagementhq.com/gcp-newmarket-road-23
